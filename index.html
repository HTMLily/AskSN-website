Frequently Asked Questions

What is Aneurysm

An aneurysm is an abnormal bulge or dilation in the wall of a blood vessel, usually an artery, caused by weakness in the vessel wall.

Signs & Symptoms of Anuerysm

Most aneurysms do not cause symptoms until they rupture. Some warning signs of an unruptured aneurysm include headaches, blurred vision, neck pain, and dizziness. Symptoms of a ruptured aneurysm include sudden, severe headache, nausea and vomiting, stiff neck, and loss of consciousness.

What Causes an Aneurysm

The exact cause of an aneurysm is not known, but risk factors include high blood pressure, atherosclerosis, smoking, and a family history of aneurysms.
